The Ethical Crisis

Love at What Cost?

As time spent near dogs increases, humans gradually lose their ability to perceive color. Yet faced with this knowledge, most refuse to give up their pets — tolerating personal harm and endangering those around them. Eye-bies does not just steal your sight. It reveals where humanity's loyalties truly lie.

Society fractures between those who see dogs as family and those who see them as the enemy. No consensus. No easy answer.

Transmission

How It Spreads

The disease originates from a bacterial mutation in dogs' water bowls on the island. Three primary transmission pathways have been identified from survivor accounts:

💧

Contaminated water bowl bacteria transferred through a dog licking near the owner's eyes

🐾

Physical contact alone — prolonged touching caused a burning sensation until color perception ceased

🩺

Veterinarians infected through oral and organ contact during surgery — no protective gear proved sufficient

The Island

Origin & Escape

On a remote island in the Atlantic Ocean, a silent mutation spread through the canine population. No visible symptoms in the dogs. No warning for the humans.

When the outbreak was understood, the community made a devastating choice: abandon every dog on the island and flee by boat. But as the boats crossed the ocean, survivors began to realize — the disease had already crossed with them.

With dwindling food and impossible seas, only a handful reached North America's coast. They brought with them the only record of what happened: their memories, and their gray, colorless world.

Competing Theories

What People Believe

Scientists have no confirmed cause. Into that vacuum, survivor stories have bred two dominant theories — and the argument between them has become as contagious as the disease itself.

Theory 01 — Saliva Mutation

The bacteria mutated specifically in dogs' saliva. Only direct oral contact — particularly near the eyes or mouth — can transmit the disease. Casual proximity is safe.

Theory 02 — Full Contact Spread

Any point of bodily contact can transmit the bacteria through skin absorption. There is no safe distance. All physical contact with infected dogs is dangerous.

"Everything went gray."

This is what the survivors of the island say. Not that they went blind. Not that they lost their sight. Just that one day, without warning — the color was gone.

Canine Achromatopsia-26 remains uncontained. An antidote has not been found. The island has been quarantined. Scientists continue to dispute the mechanism of spread. The survivors are still gray.
